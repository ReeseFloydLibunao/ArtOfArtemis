The Hunting Goddess' Beginnings

Artemis’ Origins were as complicated as many other Major Greek gods, which means Zeus gave birth to them out of cheating on Hera with someone else. In this case, he cheated on Hera with Leto which Hera did not take likely, once she got word she did everything in her power to make her suffer. Hera first started by cursing Leto, that she would not give birth anywhere under the sun, not on any land nor island would she give birth; Leto in return was forced to wander the earth until she found a place to give birth. In one version she talks to an island and the island agrees to help her as long as her son, Apollo, had a temple built on the island, in another, Poseidon, seeing all of this, had pity for Leto and made her an island named Delos so that she could give birth on it without being under Hera’s curse. And in some more, obscure versions they are chased by a giant snake called Python who A. heard that Leto was pregnant with Zeus’ child and B. That Apollo would kill him in battle, which caused the Python to chase after Leto. He was unsuccessful and was eventually slain by Apollo.

Leto, after finally resting beneath an olive tree, gave birth to the Twin Gods Artemis and Apollo, in some versions Artemis was a sort of “midwife” to Leto as she had just given birth. Being a god, Artemis aged very quickly already being a teenager in the first week of being alive. Yet despite being so young, she already knew what she wanted and who to get it from, she wanted to be a goddess of the hunt, she wanted a bow, a pack of hunting dogs, a group of nymphs and dryads to essentially be her friend group and to never be married. Who to get it from? Zeus. Upon seeing Zeus she immediately got on his lap and stated all of her demands, which Zeus could not resist fulfilling, though she still had to get them herself. Like going to Hephaestus with his Cyclopes to get her bow or going to Pan to get her dogs. But still, she succeeded, and ascended to Olympus.

With this, she established and expanded her domains of Hunt, Wilderness, Chastity, Childbirth and etc. She also became, like Apollo, a deity to a celestial body, hers being the Moon while Apollo took the sun, though Selene and Helios still are the physical embodiment of the Moon and Sun, Artemis and Apollo can still somewhat reside over those. She was also a guardian to children and their mothers, she could ensure a childbirth either went successfully or painfully, and she often protected maidens from pain, a guardian of sorts. For residing over these, she was both praised and loved and feared aplenty and for good reasons, further solidifying her domain across Greece.

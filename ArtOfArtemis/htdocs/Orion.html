The Tragedy Of Orion

Orion was a friendly giant in some versions of mythology, but a normal human in others. A consistent characteristic with the famous constellation is being the son of Poseidon. Born from Poseidon as the father and Euryale, the daughter of the King of Minos of Crete, as the mother to the constellation. Additionally, quite like a European deity, he was given the ability from his father to walk on water. He is most associated with both the stars and Artemis, being a very close friend and partner to Artemis, in some versions, a fiancé to the hunting goddess. He was essentially the only exception to Artemis’ only befriend girls rule. She loved him so much to a point where feelings of attraction resonated within the goddess, to a point where she’d break her vow of chastity for him. This threatened and concerned two gods, Gaia, primordial being of earth, and Apollo, Twin of Artemis and god of sunlight.

The two concerned deities decide to kill Orion not only once, but twice. But in different narratives of course, one is simpler than the other, specifically Gaia’s version. Where when Gaia learned of Artemis possibly breaking chastity and purity because of this giant and his influence over her. She sent a giant scorpion in order to kill the poor giant. He put up a good fight but he died nonetheless. Out of pity, Gaia or the gods of Olympus depending on which version you are following decided to mark the giant out in the sky along with scorpion from that slayed him. Making the Constellations of Scorpio and Orion, which Orion is fighting said scorpion.

In a separate version, a devious prank is played on the son of Poseidon by a cunning Apollo. Where once he received word of the possibility that his dear sister would ever consider breaking the vow of chastity over a son of his, at times, annoying uncle Poseidon, even though as gods most of them can barely keep it in their pants, he couldn’t bear the thought, and rose to action. Whether out of pity or a sick joke. He first saw Orion swimming in the water, and an idea struck him, one that doesn’t involve him shooting Orion himself, but instead, his sister would do the killing for him. He challenged Artemis to hit the far off object in the water, as a Goddess, she expertly hit the target with utmost precision. She only realized once his body washed up ashore that it was her dear friend Orion. And of course, just like in the first story he was made into a constellation out of pity.
